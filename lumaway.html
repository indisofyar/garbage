Lumaway onboarding

Improved onboarding for a student mentoring platform.

Overview

Lumaway is a platform tailored for high school and university to allow them to explore career paths and connect with student and industry mentors. In a team of 4 UX Designers, I worked with the organisation and a senior UX mentor to explore how to improve Lumaway's onboarding journey.

Project Details

Purpose: University project.

Role: UX/UI Design, information architecture and user research.

Tools: Google Forms, Maze and Figma.

Design Brief

Lumaway was in the process of partnering with Student Edge, an organisation which provides exclusive student discounts, a move which would provide them with acccess to a much greater base of high school student users. As a result, the project focused on how to connect with this user base in particular. Several deliverables were initially discussed including: medium fidelity prototypes mapping out an onboarding journey, a few phone screens how the platform would work on mobile, an information architecture diagram, and user research justifying design decisions.

User Research

Survey

Our team conducted a user survey on 43 unique respondents.

Key findings

Areas of study dominated by Commerce/Business and Medicine; others included languages, vet science, drama.

72.7% indicated familiarites with careers platforms.

A significant portion of respondents also expressed positive associations with personalised journeys, many desiring a quick onboarding journey, and many being already familiar with other careers platforms.

User Persona

John Maguire

John is in his final year of High School. He's focussing on getting the best marks he can, but still doesn't know which universities he wants to apply for. John thinks he wants to go into the tech field in the future, but doesn't know what his options are past the typical engineering and computer science degrees.

Goals

Learn about the types of roles in the tech space.

Choosing a uni degree to study.

Frustrations

Clutter of irrelevant information.

Decision Fatigue.

Motivators

A variety of specific roles.

Language and interfaces tailored to her age demographic.
