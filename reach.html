Reach App

The app which connects outreach workers.

Problem space

Outreach workers provide support, referrals and education to individuals in need across Australia. They work for different organisations such as The Court Network, SESLHD and others. They often undergo a high degree of training and work closely with others to improve outreach skills. With 62,200 Australian currently employed as Welfare Support Workers (Australian Government Job Outlook, 2020) and many of them conducting outreach, this is a significant financial burden for the organisations involved.

Solution

Reach app is intended to help increase operational efficiency for these organisations by providing outreach workers with increased access to training and information sharing with the aim of facilitating improved outreach abilities for these workers. Through this, organisations which conduct outreach can train eachother’s workers and reduce the amount of training session’s they’ll have to organise.

Project Details

Purpose: University project

Role: UX/UI Design, literature research and stakeholder interviews.

Tools: Adobe Illustrator, Adobe After Effects and Figma

Discovery Phase

What were the key issues faced by outreach workers?

Interviews

Video call interviews with 8 stakeholders, that were outreach workers and outreach worker managerial staff, helped me understand what issues they faced. Gaining both perspectives, I believed, was vital to creating a design solution which could be realistically implemented and also address user needs.

Key research insights

I derived several key takeaways from this research.

Facilite the flow of information

There are many benefits for outreach workers by facilitating the flow of information between different organisations. Although not always appropriate, facilitating information and training for outreach workers could enhance the rate at which outreach workers are successful in providing outreach and could improve factors such as communication styles, conflict resolution and knowledge sharing.

Create more opportunities for understanding

Reflective practices can be effective for outreach. Indeed, due to many outreach workers dealing with complex situations, allowing outreach workers to have more opportunities about how to approach situations, why some interactions were better than others, and what can be done in future could assist workers significantly. This could have the impact of allowing workers to reach more clients more effectively.

Providing outreach can be complex

Outreach method which benefits one group - such as using virtual information distribution - may not necessarily benefit another. For instance, many indigenous groups use technology to lesser degree, in which case, distributing virtual resources would be ineffective. Indeed, not every tool or resource will be appropriate for every outreach worker, so ensuring the right resources and trainings get to the right workers will be a key consideration.

User Persona

I created a user persona as to create a focus during the design process on what to develop.

Bob Smith

Bob is a compassionate outreach worker at the HIV and Related Programs Unit with limited experience performing outreach prior to beginning the program. He cares about social justice issues and helping others.

Negative trends

Bob works a casual job so he can often forget his training due to the work being sporadic.

Positive trends

Outreach workers often have significant training prior to commencing work.

Bob's supervisor is highly compassionate and supportive.

Barriers

Uninterested clients.

Negatively impacting clients' mental health.

Needs

Training and support.

Opportunities for connecting with others.

Opportunities

Access to more training opportunities.

Better access to post-outreach reflection.

Greater ability to communicate mishaps.
